Steppy steps

  1. Set a seed so that the data is reproducible later.
set.seed(1337)
  1. Create a vector that contains 100 randomly sampled values from a negative binomial distribution.
neg_bin_dist_samples <- rnbinom(n = 100, mu = 10, size = 2)
head(neg_bin_dist_samples)
[1]  5 11  9 25 21  8
  1. Create a vector that contains log10-transformed values from the vector in step 1.
neg_bin_dist_samples_log10 <- log(neg_bin_dist_samples+1)
head(neg_bin_dist_samples_log10)
[1] 1.791759 2.484907 2.302585 3.258097 3.091042 2.197225
  1. Plot histograms of raw and log10-transformed data side by side
par(mfrow = c(1,2))

hist(neg_bin_dist_samples,
     main='Raw data')
hist(neg_bin_dist_samples_log10,
     main='Log10-transform data')

  1. Display the mean and the variance of the obtained data.
  • Raw data:
mean_raw <- summary(neg_bin_dist_samples)["Mean"]
var_raw <- var(neg_bin_dist_samples)

Therefore we obtain a mean of 10.23 and a variance of 62.9667677 for our randomly sampled data.

  • Log10-transformed data:
mean_log10 <- summary(neg_bin_dist_samples_log10)["Mean"]
var_log10 <- var(neg_bin_dist_samples_log10)

Therefore we obtain a mean of 2.1601898 and a variance of 0.5866858 for our log10-transformed data.
